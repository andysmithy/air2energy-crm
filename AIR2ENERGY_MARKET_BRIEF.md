## CORE TECHNOLOGY & VALUE PROPOSITION

### The Problem
- Hard-to-abate industries face rising fuel costs, tightening emissions regulations, and lack of commercially viable alternatives
- Australia operates **160,000+ industrial boilers** producing 12 tonnes of CO₂/day each (4,380 tonnes/year per boiler)
- One cargo ship burns $34M in fuel annually; global shipping emits 1 billion tonnes CO₂/year
- LNG prices have increased **500%** over the past 10 years, squeezing operating margins
- Companies are stuck in lose-lose situation: either pollute and pay carbon penalties, or adopt expensive, unproven alternatives

### The Solution
**Retrofit electrochemical system** that:
- Attaches directly to existing gas boiler/ship exhaust (no replacement needed, no shutdown)
- Filters CO₂ from exhaust stream
- Uses electrochemical reaction in fuel cell to convert CO₂ to **usable electricity**
- Works in **high-emission environments** with NOx, SOx, particulates, condensation ("all the nasties")
- Seamless design maintains exhaust pressure—no disruption to combustion assets

### Competitive Differentiation
- **Key advantage:** Can operate in harsh exhaust conditions with impurities (competitors require pre-treatment/separate facilities)
- **Integration:** Single integrated system (no need to transport/store carbon elsewhere)
- **Energy production:** Generates electricity from emissions (vs. capture-only approaches)
- **Payback model:** System designed to pay itself back in **2.5-8 years** based on energy production value
- **Retrofit-first:** Works with existing infrastructure (vs. requiring full system replacement)
